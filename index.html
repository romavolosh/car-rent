Booking a Rental Car in Paphos: Step-by-Step Guide

Booking a car in Paphos is straightforward if you follow these steps to ensure a smooth process.

1

Plan Your Trip

Determine your travel dates, the number of passengers, and the type of vehicle you need. Consider your itinerary—will you stick to Paphos or venture to other parts of Cyprus, like Limassol or Larnaca?

2

Book in Advance

Booking your car at least a few weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons (June–August), ensures better availability and lower prices. Online platforms allow you to compare options and secure your vehicle before arriving.

3

Check Requirements

Most rental companies in Paphos require a valid driver’s license (an International Driving Permit may be needed for non-EU licenses), a minimum age of 21 (some companies charge a young driver fee for those under 25), and a credit card for the security deposit.

4

Review the Rental Agreement

Read the terms and conditions carefully. Pay attention to insurance details, mileage limits, and cancellation policies. Ask about additional fees for extras like GPS, child seats, or additional drivers.

5

Inspect the Vehicle

Before driving off, inspect the car for any existing damage and ensure it’s noted in the rental agreement. Take photos of the vehicle for your records to avoid disputes when returning it.

Driving in Paphos: Tips and Regulations

Driving in Paphos is generally straightforward, but understanding local rules and road conditions is essential for a safe experience.

Driving Rules in Cyprus

Drive on the Left

Cyprus follows British driving rules, so vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Speed Limits

Urban areas: 50 km/h; highways: 100 km/h; motorways: 120 km/h.

Seatbelts and Safety

Seatbelts are mandatory for all passengers. The legal blood alcohol limit is 0.05%. Avoid drinking and driving.

Mobile Phones

Using a handheld phone while driving is illegal; use hands-free devices if necessary.

Road Conditions

Paphos roads are generally well-maintained, but rural areas may have narrower or unpaved roads. Be cautious when driving in the Troodos Mountains or the Akamas Peninsula, where roads can be winding or rocky.

Parking in Paphos

Parking is widely available in Paphos, with paid parking zones in the city center and free parking in less busy areas. Look for blue lines indicating paid parking and use parking apps or machines to pay. Avoid parking in yellow-lined zones, as these are reserved for loading/unloading.

Fuel Stations

Fuel stations are plentiful in Paphos, with both petrol and diesel options. Most stations accept credit cards, but smaller ones may require cash. Fuel prices in Cyprus are moderate, typically ranging from €1.40 to €1.70 per liter.

Top Destinations to Explore with Your Rental Car

A rental car unlocks the best of Paphos and beyond. Here are some must-visit destinations:

  • Paphos Archaeological Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park features ancient Roman villas with stunning mosaics. It’s just a short drive from Paphos city center.
  • Aphrodite’s Rock (Petra tou Romiou): Legend says this is the birthplace of Aphrodite. Located 25 km from Paphos, it’s a scenic drive along the coast with breathtaking views.
  • Coral Bay: A popular beach with golden sands and calm waters, Coral Bay is perfect for families and water sports enthusiasts. It’s just 12 km from Paphos.
  • Akamas Peninsula: This rugged, unspoiled region is ideal for nature lovers. Explore hiking trails, visit Lara Beach (a turtle nesting site), or take a dip in the Blue Lagoon. A 4x4 is recommended for this area.
  • Troodos Mountains: A 1.5-hour drive from Paphos, the Troodos Mountains offer cooler temperatures, charming villages, and monasteries like Kykkos. Perfect for a day trip.
